Radicals

A radical is someone on the extreme left of the political spectrum. They typically believe in communism and have a strong desire for change, to the point of being willing to go to extreme measures to attain it.

Moderates

A moderate is someone in the middle of the political spectrum. They have little to no prefererence for either side, and don't care about the amount of change being done.

Reactionaries

A reactionary is someone on the extreme right of the politcal spectrum. They hate change and want to preserve traditional values as much as possible, to the point of wanting to "go back in time".

Communism

Communism is an ideology that aims to abolish private property, social classes, and eventually government. It is a response to classical liberalism and aims to create a fair society where everyone is truly equal.

There are many different variants of communism but the most popular is marxism, conceptualized by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels in The Communist Manifesto.

Socialism

Socialism is an ideology that argues for more public property and giving control of resources to the government.

Although socialism is similar to communism, it doesn't aim to fully abolish private property or social classes.

Individualism

Individualism is an ideology that puts emphasis on individuals, prioritizing the individual over the state.

It aims to give individuals more freedom and choice, regardless if it is beneficial to society as a whole.

Fascism

Fascism is an ideology characterized by ultranationalism, social hierarchy, and suppression of individualism. It promotes an authoritarian government, often led by a single ruling party.

Karl Marx

Karl Marx was a philosopher most well known for conceptualizing Marxism and writing The Communist Manifesto.

Thomas Hobbes

Thomas Hobbes was a philosopher who believed that humans were inherently violent and selfish. He believed that authority was required, even at the cost of freedom, to maintain order and security.

John Locke

John Locke was a philosopher who believed that humans were not inherently good or bad, but rather born as a blank slate influenced by external factors. While he believed humans had a slight disposition towards being good, he argued that external factors were what ultimately determined a person's beliefs and behaviour.

Jean-Jacques Rousseau

Jean-Jacques Rousseau was a philosopher who believed that humans were inherently good in their natural state, but developed society and its emphasis on self-interest would corrupt them over time. He believed in a direct democracy, where citizens vote on issues and policies instead of voting for representatives to decide on their behalf.

Adam Smith

Adam Smith, often called "The Father of Capitalism," was a philosopher known for his ideas about free markets and self-interest. He believed in "The Invisible Hand," the idea that when individuals act in their own self-interest they inevitably end up benefitting society as whole.

Classical Liberalism

Classical Liberalism is an ideology that emphasizes individual freedom, free markets, and the rule of law. It was largely a result of the Enlightenment and the growing dissatisfaction of the monarchy.

Modern Liberalism

Modern Liberalism is an ideolgy which builds on Classical Liberalism, still emphaszizing individual freedom but allowing more government intervention in order to reduce inequality.
